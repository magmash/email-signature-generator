How to Update Your Email Signature

After copying your signature using the button above, follow the steps for your email client:

Gmail
  1. Open Gmail in your web browser.
  2. Click the Settings gear icon (‚öôÔ∏è) in the top right, then click "See all settings".
  3. Scroll down to the "Signature" section.
  4. If you have an existing signature, hover over it and click the Edit (pencil) icon, or click "+ Create new" to add one.
  5. Give your signature a name (e.g., "Default").
  6. In the signature editing box, delete any existing content.
  7. Paste your copied signature (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V) directly into the box. It should appear with the correct formatting.
  8. Below the editor, ensure your new signature is selected for "FOR NEW EMAILS USE" and optionally for "ON REPLY/FORWARD USE".
  9. Scroll to the bottom of the Settings page and click "Save Changes".
Outlook (Desktop App - Windows/Mac)
  1. Open the Outlook desktop application.
  2. Go to File > Options (Windows) or Outlook > Preferences (Mac).
  3. Select Mail > Signatures... (Windows) or Email > Signatures (Mac).
  4. Select the email account you want to modify (if you have multiple).
  5. Click "New" to create a signature or select an existing one to edit.
  6. Give your signature a name.
  7. In the "Edit signature" box, delete any existing content.
  8. Paste your copied signature (Ctrl+V or Cmd+V) into the box.
  9. Use the dropdowns (usually on the right) to choose this signature for "New messages" and optionally for "Replies/forwards".
  10. Click OK to save and close the signature windows.

Note: Pasting formatted signatures into Outlook desktop can sometimes be inconsistent. If it doesn't look right, try copying the HTML code and pasting that if Outlook provides an HTML editing option, or simplify the formatting if needed.

Apple Mail (macOS)
  1. Open the Mail application.
  2. Go to Mail > Preferences... (or Settings... in newer macOS versions) from the menu bar.
  3. Click the Signatures tab.
  4. Select the email account you want to add the signature to in the left column.
  5. Click the "+" button below the middle column to create a new signature.
  6. Give your signature a name (e.g., "Company Standard").
  7. In the right-hand editing pane, uncheck the box that says "Always match my default message font". This is important!
  8. Delete any placeholder text in the editing pane.
  9. Paste your copied signature (Cmd+V) into the pane. It should appear formatted.
  10. Drag the signature name from the middle column onto the desired email account in the left column if it's not already assigned.
  11. Use the "Choose Signature" dropdown at the bottom to select your new signature as the default for that account.
  12. Close the Preferences/Settings window.
